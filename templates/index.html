Migraines

Chronic Migraines are a common and disabling condition reported in approximately 3.3 million people in the US alone. In the Global Burden of Disease Study by the World Health Organization, updated in 2013, migraine was found to be the sixth highest cause worldwide of years lost due to disability. Migraine attacks sometimes increase in frequency over time.

Weather Plays a Role

Weather can have a signifiacnt impact on frequency and severity of migraines for sufferers. Using the World Weather Online API, we looked at hsistorical data going back xx years.

Barometric Pressure

Although we used a number of factors, we focused in on Barometric Pressure. We are hoping to see a correlation between the change of Barometric Pressure and the onset of a migraine.

No more Yelling at Clouds like Abe Simpson

There are a number of triggers that may cause the onsent of migraines. Some of these happen days, some just moments before. We hope to relieve some of the anxiety of the unknown by providing a forecast with levels of risk associated with each day. These are based on weather conditions, a known trigger.

Machine Learning

We have trained our Machine Learning algorithim on historical weather data coupled with real world experiences from migraine sufferers. As you record and enter the dtaes of migraines the machine will tailor predictions for you.
